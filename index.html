From the EditorA note on the program and what it collects.

The Realization Engine is a program of inquiry into the coupled system of practitioner, language model, and professional work. Its subject is the conditions under which augmented human capability compounds, rather than erodes, when brought to bear on the work of a knowledge profession.

The projects collected here instantiate the program for specific professions. F-Star publishes the general framework. Spec Chat instantiates it for software engineering, through a specification language and a validator exposed as a Model Context Protocol server. ASAP prepares the instantiation for enterprise architecture. The pattern extends to other professions as they undertake their own instantiations.

Entries publish as they stabilize. Source is open. Revisions are frequent. Arguments are tested against the artifacts they describe.
